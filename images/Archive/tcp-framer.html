The Connected Parent

Health/EdTech | C2C SaaS | Responsive | Branding

The Connected Parent (TCP) is an online SaaS platform that helps families connect with disability, autism, and mental health resources. The searchable directory allows families to filter results based on their child's needs and find relevant services.

Role

Lead Product Designer

Duration

9 months

Tools

Figma, Illustrator, Procreate

Skills

User Research, Task Flows, Prototyping, Usability Testing, Interaction Design

Problem

Users struggled with account creation, search, and leaving reviews. Colors did not meet accessibility standards, creating usability and compliance issues.

Goals

Simplify navigation and workflows, redesign search and filters, and introduce an accessible color palette while keeping the brand playful.

Research & Insights

To understand user needs, I surveyed 15 families and evaluated the existing site. Insights revealed reliance on peer reviews, difficulty navigating flows, and frustration with inaccessible visuals.

71%

Most influenced by reviews from other families.

67%

Have family members needing help across all tasks.

57%

Attend 5–10 support events per year.

Evaluating the Existing Website

Original TCP site markup with ADA and flow issues.

Placeholder: Redesigned search UI with clear filters and accessible colors.

Design Process

Sketching & Flows

Early sketches and flows explored ways to simplify account creation and resource search. Rapid iterations helped validate direction with the client.

Prototyping & Testing

Interactive prototypes in Figma allowed usability testing. Feedback guided refinements in navigation, search filters, and review workflows.

Outcomes

Improved UX

Navigation simplified and flows clarified for key actions.

Accessibility

New color palette meets WCAG standards while staying playful.

Engagement

Families can now contribute and read reviews more easily.

“The redesign finally makes TCP usable for our families — they can find and share resources without confusion.”

Reflection

This project was deeply personal, shaped by my own family’s experience with disability support. Working with TCP reinforced the value of accessibility and inclusive design. Future iterations will expand into community features, building on a strong foundation of usability and trust.
