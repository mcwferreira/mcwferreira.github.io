Uspire

B2C | Startup | Mobile

A B2C mobile application that directly links students with study abroad advisors and prepares them for what comes ahead with personalized guidance and support.

ROLE

Product Designer

DURATION

3 months

TOOLS

Figma, Illustrator, Miro

SKILLS

User Research, Task Flows, Prototyping, Usability Testing, Interaction Design

research

Project Kick-off

At the outset, our team convened for a kickoff call to familiarize ourselves with the Uspire project's scope. During this session, we carefully reviewed the materials provided by the client, which encompassed resources such as a video showcasing the Uspire desktop app, a style guide, and a list of similar platforms. This opportunity also allowed us to formulate additional inquiries for the client, aiming to gain deeper insights into Uspire, its features, objectives, and design inspirations. This first step played a pivotal role in helping us identify the forthcoming challenges and tailor our design process to align with the specific needs and objectives of the Uspire project.

Understanding the Client's Goals

Our client engagement involved thorough questioning to ensure a precise understanding of their vision. Upon receiving comprehensive responses, the project gained clarity, igniting our enthusiasm for the design process.

Key insights from our discussions:
1. Minimalist and Simple Design: The client's strong emphasis on minimalism and simplicity in design was evident. They provided us with brands that served as inspiration, allowing us to align our creative direction with their preferences.
2. Advisor-Student fit: Our client stressed the importance of connecting students with the right advisors for their study abroad journey, emphasizing personalized support.
3. Comprehensive Exam Prep: Beyond exam readiness, the client aimed to provide students with a holistic approach to academic success, covering study strategies and support.

Competitive Analysis

To gain a better understanding of similar, existing sites' strengths and weaknesses in existing solutions, a competitive analysis was conducted. This helped guide the design to ensure it would be a standout user experience that truly meets the needs of students and advisors.

  • User Onboarding Effectiveness: Our analysis revealed variations in user registration and onboarding experiences across competing websites, highlighting areas of potential improvement.
  • Search and Navigation Efficiency: Our evaluation of competing platforms uncovered variances in user-friendliness, pinpointing potential usability advantages found within Uspire.
  • Advisor Matching Performance: An analysis of competitors' platforms revealed varying degrees of success in matching students with advisors based on their needs and goals.
  • Community and Social Integration Strategies: Our analysis examined how competitors foster community interaction and social integration among students and advisors.
  • Study Abroad Resources and Guidance Assessment: Our examination of competitive platforms scrutinized the availability and usefulness of study abroad resources and guidance.

User Stories

With the scope outlined by the client and a clear set of goals, we worked to craft user stories for the site that focused on the key points that our client wanted our designs to encompass.

Key insights from our discussions:
1. As a user (counselor), I want to be able to create an account and log in
2. As a user, (counselor), I want to be able to showcase my services/plans, reviews, and testimonials.
3. As a user, (counselor), I want to be able to accept requests from a student and schedule initial intro calls.
4. As a user (counselor), I want to be able to send offers to the client (student)

Ideation

User Flows

Having solidified our user stories, we proceeded to craft user flows as a strategic pathway for users to achieve their objectives. These user flows served as a visual guide, illustrating the steps users need to take while accommodating various choices they may encounter along the journey. I was tasked with completing user story 4 for this phase of the project.

Medium Fidelity Wireframes

To begin the ideation process, we split up into teams and worked on expanding the user stories and developing wireframes.

I started creating medium-fidelity wireframes for sending an offer to a potential client. For this, I ensured that my wireframes facilitated a clearer understanding of the user journey and interactions.

Design

Inspiration and Exploration

We delved into competitor products that embodied the client's vision of modernity, minimalism, and simplicity, with a particular focus on aspects relevant to notifications, lists, calendars, and dashboards.

Throughout this research journey, the team gleaned valuable insights into the components and styles employed by these competitors. These insights, in turn, played a pivotal role in shaping these crucial elements of the final design, ensuring that our approach aligned seamlessly with the client's desired aesthetics and best practices for user experience.

UI Iterations

After gaining inspiration and a clear vision for our design, we prioritized the dashboard screen as the initial focus for our high-fidelity design efforts. Each team member crafted separate iterations of this screen, exploring various color schemes and layouts.

We presented three final design options to the client, with the middle option being the selected choice. This iteration demonstrated simplicity, conciseness, and a perfect alignment with the client's vision.

Hi-Fidelity Wireframes

It was a quick transition from mid-fis to hi-fis with the completed style guide, component library, and approved UI iteration from the client. I worked again on user flow 4: As a user (counselor), I want to be able to send offers to the client (student).
The output goals for the hi-fi designs were:

  • Enhanced Clarity: I refined the screens from med-fi to ensure a crystal-clear process for counselors to send offers to students, eliminating any potential ambiguity or confusion.
  • Streamlined Navigation: I optimized the navigation within the flow, reducing the number of steps required to complete the task, and making it more efficient and user-friendly.
  • Improved Information Hierarchy: I fine-tuned the layout to emphasize key information, ensuring that essential details were prominently displayed, making it easy for both counselors and students to grasp the offer's significance.
  • Established Color Palette: I harmonized with our updated colors, ensuring that the screens remained minimal yet visually appealing, creating a cohesive and aesthetically pleasing experience throughout the offer-sending process.
