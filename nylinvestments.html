Challenge & Goals

Problem

Investment concepts are complex. Pages were content‑heavy, tool usage was uneven, and conversions from education pages to sign‑up lagged. Updates had to fit within AEM templates and pass legal review.

Goals

Make data easier to understand, reduce friction for key actions (calculate, subscribe, contact), and standardize components so authors can ship consistently in AEM.

Feature Focus — Data Explainers

Context

Users skim long content and miss key takeaways. We designed a reusable Data Explainer module: a headline insight, a concise chart, and a one‑sentence interpretation — all accessible and responsive.

Approach

  • Standard chart patterns (line, bar, donut) with consistent legends and annotations.
  • Keyboard‑navigable tabs to switch views (e.g., 1Y/5Y/10Y) without page reloads.
  • Alt text + data tables for screen readers and legal audit.

Feature Focus — Sign‑up Modal

Problem

Conversion points were buried. Email capture sat at the bottom of articles and returned weak opt‑in rates. Legal copy and disclosures made the form feel heavy.

Solution

  • Intent‑based trigger (end of article or after calculator use) instead of instant pop‑ups.
  • One‑field start (email) → progressive disclosure for preferences & disclosures.
  • Accessible focus states, escape to close, and clear value prop.

AEM Operations & Componentization

What made this tricky

Enterprise constraints: locked templates, multiple product lines, and legal review. We built within AEM’s component model so authors could reuse patterns without custom dev every time.

How we worked

  • Defined content models for calculators, charts, and sign‑ups with clear authoring fields.
  • Documented do/don’t examples and added inline helper text for authors.
  • Created a sandbox page type for quick QA before publishing.
