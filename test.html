The future of Casa hardware

Casa hardware — concept prototype
By Rachel Chen
·
Oct 22, 2025

This project explores a compact home hardware ecosystem with a focus on approachable software, physical form, and an app that helps families maintain devices and share access quickly.

We started with a small set of constraints: the hardware needed to be inexpensive, installable without tools, and the app flow had to be simple enough for non-technical household members. Over several sprints we converged on a modular product and a mobile-first app experience.

We built quick prototypes using 3D-printed casings and off-the-shelf sensors. The early tests prioritized mounting, power, and discoverability.

Prototype A — clip-on sensor
Prototype B — adhesive puck

Discoverability was a recurring problem. We experimented with a minimal pairing flow that relied on proximity and pictured prompts so users could follow instructions quickly.

We emphasized three flows: install, invite, and routine automation. Each flow was reduced to no more than three screens to lower cognitive load.

We also created small marketing photography sets to show devices in-context. Those shots were used across the product page and the app onboarding flows.

Design note: Keep the app flows short and image-forward. Use real photos of the devices in-context for onboarding to increase trust and reduce confusion.
