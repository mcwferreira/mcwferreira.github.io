Overview

SupplyLogic, a leading provider of marketing execution and supply chain solutions, needed a website that reflected its new brand identity and technology-driven services. The redesign focused on improving usability, clarifying complex offerings, and strengthening trust with enterprise clients across industries.

Research

To align the new site with SupplyLogic's rebrand and enterprise positioning, I ran a pragmatic research sprint focused on clarifying messaging and improving usability for marketing and procurement audiences.

Methods

  • Stakeholder interviews with sales, marketing, and operations leads
  • Content audit of the existing site to identify messaging gaps
  • Heuristic UX audit to capture navigation, hierarchy, and accessibility issues
  • Competitive scan of B2B/martech websites for messaging and trust patterns
  • Lightweight user validation — 4–6 short interviews with marketing/procurement leads

Key findings

  • Unclear value proposition — visitors couldn't tell platform vs. service at a glance
  • Dense pages and weak scanability
  • Navigation did not provide clear audience-specific paths
  • Low presence of trust signals and case examples
  • Accessibility and responsive issues to address

Design implications

  • Simplify hero messaging + create clear CTAs and audience entry points
  • Reorganize content into scannable sections (Platform, Services, Proof, How we work)
  • Introduce a component-based design system for consistency
  • Add trust signals (client logos, short case blurbs, KPIs) and ensure WCAG compliance
  • Run quick follow-up tests to validate comprehension and CTA performance

Reflection

Designing this app held a deep personal significance for me because my brother-in-law has a disability in which he requires comprehensive support in his daily life. Because of this, I have witnessed firsthand the challenges and obstacles involved in accessing disability resources, particularly for adults. This drove my passion to assist the client in enhancing their existing website to better serve users.

Embarking on this project, I found great excitement in my client's enthusiastic approach. It became clear to me that prioritizing the user's needs and ensuring the brand's identity aligned with its function were of utmost importance. Throughout the process, there were numerous obstacles, such as determining the right colors for the brand, which required multiple discussions with the client. However, the end result is a design that is inclusive and user-friendly, and it fills me with pride.

Looking ahead, the design has the potential to expand as additional features, including a social platform, are incorporated into the website. I am eager to further refine and develop these ideas, building upon the foundation we have laid he final design of The Connected Parent platform offers an enhanced user experience, improved accessibility, and a refined visual identity. This project reinforced the importance of user-centered design principles in creating impactful digital solutions.
