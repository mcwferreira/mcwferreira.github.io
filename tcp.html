The Connected Parent

Health/EdTech | C2C SaaS | Startup | Responsive | Branding

The Connected Parent (TCP) is an online SaaS platform that helps families connect with the disability, autism, and mental health resources they need. The searchable directory allows families to easily filter results based on their child's particular needs to find a wide range of services.

a brief overview

Problem

The Connected Parent is a newly launched site that requires refinement to many of its primary features and overall design. While there has been a lot of effort put into ensuring that the back-end of the platform is market ready, there were concerns regarding the usability of the front-end of the site.

Specifically, users expressed confusion with several primary features including how to create an account, navigate the search functionality, and write reviews for businesses.

There was also concern about the color palette being used because the platform did not meet accessibility guidelines. The client expressed that they were open to adjusting color selections, but wanted to maintain a playful and inviting style.

Solution

I updated The Connected Parent's website to meet the following goals:

  • Redesign the search function and filters to hold a more prominent position on the platform, and provide a more versatile interface for the search filters so they can be easily adapted as the needs of the users change.

  • Develop a style guide updated with new color options that meet accessibility guidelines and remain closely aligned with the brand's original positioning.

  • Optimize workflows to make website navigation easy and intuitive for users, considering the placement and functionality of menus and navigation bars, the ease of access to important pages, and the simplicity of the overall design.

Evaluation

The evaluation of The Connected Parent website revealed several critical issues that require attention and refinement. The findings indicated challenges related to accessibility, user interface, navigation, and essential functionalities. The client had acknowledged the need for improvements and expressed a willingness to modify the color palette while maintaining a playful and inviting style.

Issues identified during the evaluation:

  • Does not meet WCAG accessibility guidelines in color choices
  • Unintuitive and confusing user interface
  • Unclear and complicated navigation
  • Difficulty in creating an account
  • Challenges in utilizing the search functionality
  • Complications in writing reviews for businesses

ideation

User Stories

To gain deeper insights into potential user challenges, I crafted user stories. These stories not only facilitated my understanding of the product from the user's perspective but also helped identify essential features and functionalities.

  • As a user, I want to effortlessly create an account
  • As a user, I want a user-friendly search functionality that allows me to easily find relevant and valuable resources for my needs
  • As a user, I desire the ability to both contribute my own reviews and read reviews from fellow users, providing me with valuable insights and experiences shared by the community
  • As a user, I wish to have the option to add a resource that I have discovered
  • As a user, I aim to have the option to discover events in my local area

Sketching out ideas

Understanding the needs and pain points of users was crucial in shaping the design direction. I explored a range of early ideas through quick sketches to rapidly iterate and visualize potential solutions.

This allowed me to efficiently discuss directions with the client and ensure that the evolving vision aligned with their goals. These low-fidelity sketches also helped validate that new user flows would integrate well with the existing content and accommodate real user scenarios.

Design

To ensure the design met user expectations, I conducted usability tests and gathered feedback. Iterations were made to improve the experience based on real user interactions.

Reflection

Designing this app held a deep personal significance for me because my brother-in-law has a disability in which he requires comprehensive support in his daily life. Because of this, I have witnessed firsthand the challenges and obstacles involved in accessing disability resources, particularly for adults. This drove my passion to assist the client in enhancing their existing website to better serve users.

Embarking on this project, I found great excitement in my client's enthusiastic approach. It became clear to me that prioritizing the user's needs and ensuring the brand's identity aligned with its function were of utmost importance. Throughout the process, there were numerous obstacles, such as determining the right colors for the brand, which required multiple discussions with the client. However, the end result is a design that is inclusive and user-friendly, and it fills me with pride.

Looking ahead, the design has the potential to expand as additional features, including a social platform, are incorporated into the website. I am eager to further refine and develop these ideas, building upon the foundation we have laid he final design of The Connected Parent platform offers an enhanced user experience, improved accessibility, and a refined visual identity. This project reinforced the importance of user-centered design principles in creating impactful digital solutions.
